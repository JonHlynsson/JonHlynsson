Yesterday, I was pleased to be a guest on [Yvonne Höller's](https://www.unak.is/english/moya/ugla/staff/yvonne-holler "Yvonne Höller's") podcast, [Cognitive Neuroscience @UNAK](https://open.spotify.com/show/4juMUjUn9aLJNJUfXJXJkj "Cognitive Neuroscience @UNAK"). She invited me to discuss a [study](https://doi.org/10.1556/2054.2024.00337 "study") my co-authors and I recently published on the potential negative effects of psilocybin in the context of therapy. I have previously disseminated these results in my post "_[Psychedelic-assisted therapy: Are we ignoring potential pitfalls?](https://jonhlynsson.com/psychedelic-assisted-therapy-are-we-ignoring-potential-pitfalls/ "Psychedelic-assisted therapy: Are we ignoring potential pitfalls?")_" but discussing them with Yvonne was lots of fun!

Our discussion included an overview of the field of psychedelic sciences with an emphasis on the quality of evidence for psychedelic-assisted therapy, the results of the [study](https://doi.org/10.1556/2054.2024.00337 "study"), but also psychotherapy in general, how one can become accredited as a psychologist in Iceland, and more!

Yvonne is a Professor at the Faculty of Psychology at the University of Akureyri in Iceland, and I have been fortunate to collaborate with her on the [EPIC-SAD](https://www.unak.is/english/research/research-projects/epic-sad-study-1 "EPIC-SAD") project this winter. Her thought-provoking questions and interest in science will hopefully help disseminate our study's findings and promote public discourse.

You can listen to our discussion [here](https://open.spotify.com/episode/7pX7yAHz5NcFBXKWeLFPCe?si=W8nmBf-XTLK0YF9uqlovew) or in the player below. I hope you enjoy the podcast as much as I enjoyed recording it!

https://open.spotify.com/episode/7pX7yAHz5NcFBXKWeLFPCe?si=W8nmBf-XTLK0YF9uqlovew

The show notes for the podcast are available [here](https://zenodo.org/doi/10.5281/zenodo.11094179), with links to some of the other studies we discussed.

I had lots of fun discussing our study with Yvonne and I hope that this makes our study more approachable for all individuals interested in psychedelic-assisted therapy.
