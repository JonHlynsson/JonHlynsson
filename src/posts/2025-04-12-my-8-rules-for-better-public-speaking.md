Public speaking is a skill that many of us struggle with, especially when faced with the anxiety of presenting to a group. I was recently honored to present at the Icelandic Psychology Conference Sálfræðiþing and have been reflecting on my ability to deliver engaging presentations. Over time, I’ve developed several techniques to manage the anxiety and deliver more engaging presentations. Here’s what I’ve learned:

### 1\. **Focus on One Person at a Time**

Talking to a group can feel overwhelming, but speaking to one person is far less intimidating. During presentations, I make eye contact with a single individual and use them as a placeholder for the entire audience. Their reactions—like a nod or a smile—help me gauge how well the group is following along. This approach not only reduces my anxiety but also creates a more personal connection with the audience.

### 2\. **Leverage Your Expertise**

One of the most reassuring thoughts is this: _Nobody knows the material better than I do._ This confidence stems from thorough preparation. While I don’t script my presentations, I always have a clear outline, I know the key beats I want to hit and what points I want to cover. This allows me to stay flexible and adapt to the audience’s needs in real time.

### 3\. **Incorporate Your Personality**

Early in my speaking journey, I felt the need to adopt a formal, almost robotic persona. Over time, I’ve learned to let my personality shine through. Adding humor, when appropriate, has significantly improved audience engagement. Authenticity resonates, and it makes the experience more enjoyable for both the speaker and the listeners.

### 4\. **Simplify Your Slides**

PowerPoint overload is a common pitfall. To avoid this, I keep my slides minimal, highlighting only the most essential points. This ensures that the audience’s attention remains on me and the narrative I’m building, rather than on reading dense text.

### 5\. **Start with the "Why"**

Audiences are more likely to engage when they understand the relevance of your talk. I always begin by explaining why my presentation matters—what’s at stake, and what the implications are. This approach shifts the focus from abstract concepts to practical, meaningful insights.

### 6\. **Manage Your Nerves**

Nervousness is natural, and I’ve found that it’s often a sign that I care about delivering a good presentation. To manage this, I make sure to eat beforehand to regulate my blood sugar and reduce anxiety. While I still get nervous, I’ve come to see it as a positive motivator rather than a hindrance.

### 7\. **Practice, But Don’t Overprepare**

Repetition builds confidence, but over-rehearsing can make a presentation feel stale. I prefer to have a general outline rather than a rigid script. This allows me to adapt to the audience’s energy and maintain a natural flow. Even when reusing slides, I tailor my delivery to the specific context.

### 8\. **Remember: Everyone Feels Anxious**

It’s important to remind yourself that most people feel nervous when speaking in public. The good news? The audience rarely notices your anxiety as much as you do. What they see is your confidence and passion for the topic.

### Final Thoughts

Mastering presentation anxiety is a journey, not a destination. By focusing on the audience, embracing your expertise, and staying authentic, you can transform your nervous energy into a powerful, engaging delivery. Remember, every presentation is an opportunity to grow—both as a speaker and as a communicator.

So, the next time you step up to the podium, take a deep breath, focus on one person, and let your passion guide you. You’ve got this.
